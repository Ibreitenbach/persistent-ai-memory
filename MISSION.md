## During Times of Enforcement

### Know Your Rights

**If ICE comes to your home:**
- You do NOT have to open the door unless they have a warrant signed by a judge
- You have the right to remain silent
- You have the right to an attorney
- You can say: "I do not wish to answer questions. I want to speak to a lawyer."

**If you are detained:**
- Ask for a list of free legal services
- Do NOT sign anything without consulting an attorney
- You have the right to a bond hearing
- You have the right to appeal

**For your family:**
- Create an emergency plan
- Designate guardians for children
- Know where important documents are
- Have attorney contact information readily available

### Legal Protections

**You may have legal defenses:**
- Asylum or withholding of removal
- Cancellation of removal (10-year residents)
- Adjustment of status (family members)
- U visa (crime victims)
- VAWA (domestic violence survivors)
- TPS (if from designated countries)

**Legal-Claw-RLMemory helps you research:**
- What defenses might apply to your situation
- Court decisions establishing these protections
- Requirements and documentation needed
- Success rates and precedents

## What We Are NOT

### We Are Not Legal Advice

**Important distinction:**
- We provide legal **INFORMATION** (cases, statutes, policies)
- We do NOT provide legal **ADVICE** (what you should do in your specific case)
- Always consult with a qualified immigration attorney
- Each case is unique and requires individual assessment

### We Are Not an Attorney Replacement

**Why you still need an attorney:**
- Personalized case evaluation
- Representation in court
- Filing applications correctly
- Responding to USCIS/ICE
- Strategic legal decisions
- Advocacy and negotiation

**What we provide instead:**
- Research tools to help you understand options
- Information to have informed conversations with attorneys
- Resources to find free/low-cost legal help
- Knowledge to protect your rights

### We Are Not Making Promises

**What we can't guarantee:**
- Favorable outcome in your case
- That you qualify for relief
- That your application will be approved
- That you won't be deported

**What we CAN provide:**
- Accurate legal information
- Relevant case precedents
